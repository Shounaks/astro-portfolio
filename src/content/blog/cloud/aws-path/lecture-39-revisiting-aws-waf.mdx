<Section link="why-waf" title="Why does AWS WAF Exist?" /> 
To drive outcome for customer who build and operate workload in cloud.
The WAF provides a set of questions and design principles across 5 pillars.

<br />
<Section link="operational-excellence" title="1. OPERATIONAL EXCELLENCE" /> 
- Focus on Running & monitoring systems delivering business value & continue improving process.
- Key Topics:
    - Automating changes
    - Responding to events
    - Defining standards to manage daily Operation

<br />
<Section link="security" title="2. Security" /> 
- Protecting information & systems.
- Key Topics:
    - Confidentiality
    - Integrity of data
    - Privilage Management
    - Protecting system (from attackers)
    - Establishing controls to detect security events.

<br />
<Section link="reliablity" title="3. Reliblity" /> 
Insuring the workload peforms its intended function correctly and consistently when its expected to.

A reliliant workload quickly recovers from failure to meet business & customer demand.
Key Topics:
    - Distributed System Design
    - Recovery Planning
    - Hard to handle change.

<br />
<Section link="peformance efficiency" title="4. Reliblity" /> 
- Peformance efficiency pillar focusses on using IT & Computing pillar efficiently.
- Key Topics:
    - Selecting right resource types & size based on workload requirement

<br />
<Section link="cost-optimization" title="5. Cost Optimiazation" /> 
- Avoid unnecessary costs
- Key Topics:
    - Understand & Optimize the way money is being spent.
    - Selecting most appropriate & right number of resource types, analyzing money spent over time.
    - Avoiding spending
